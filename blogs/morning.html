Waking Up Early Morning


You probably have watched Youtube videos of this. '#BillionaireMindset'. Are you one of those who wakes up early morning? Then you must be enjoying this ritual.

Waking up early has pretty good benefits.
You can well plan the day if you wake up early. You get a lot of time before class/work.

Think of all the morning energy. You woke up at 6 am. Sun is about to rise. You move from your bed, gets to the door, slowly opens it, letting the cool breeze swirl around you, the chirpy sound of the birdsâ€¦


Take a long shower you were yearning for, sing a song and dance if you want -Just make sure you don't break your bones :).

Don't drink the coffee - sip the coffee, it tastes much better. Check your bag even if you don't want to. Learn to iron clothes by yourself. Make to-do lists every day.

No more missing the bus. No more running to the class with a brush in your pocket.

The best time to workout is in the morning. Morning workout can kickstart your day with full energy. You will feel active and no nore drowsy-sleepy faces that you drag on to your work.

Evenings can make you tired with your office work. But try which time works best for you. Some of my friends likes to workout in the evening, and some choose morning. Depends on you.

Sound great right? So how do I get up early? What's the magic formula? We all know alarm is kinda joke, because we never wake to it.

The key to waking up early is to sleep early. Yup, you read it right. The Human body needs 6-7 hours of sleep for all the energy it spent during the day. And it's simple logic you may think, who doesn't know this?!

Okay, now let's put up a plan.


Hit bed by 11pm and wake up by 6am.
Make some variations to the time set with what works best for you. Tell your mom to pour a jar of water if you didn't wake up(that's a joke).

If you woke up on the first day itself, then Great! Most probably you will not.
Because our body is accustomed to the sleep cycle we followed till today.
Breaking it is not easy. If you are determined, you will be up with some efforts within 3-4 days.

Now the harder part is sticking to this routine. Never ever make an excuse. Break it once, then it's even more harder to get back in the track. The gym bhayyas knows this very well. One day they don't show up, then the next day even more lazy they become.

Stick to the routine even if it's Sunday. Work towards your goal.
Sleep well and do well, they are interconnected.
